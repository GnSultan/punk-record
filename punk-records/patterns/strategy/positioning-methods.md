# Positioning Methods

## Core Approach
Positioning is not what you say about yourself — it's the space you occupy in someone's mind. Define it intentionally or the market defines it for you.

## Key Principles
- Find the angle that's true AND unique — not just one or the other
- Position against a real alternative, not a straw man
- Use the customer's language, not industry jargon
- In African markets, local credibility often beats global polish

## Tanzanian Context Adjustments
- Word-of-mouth and WhatsApp sharing outweigh traditional marketing
- Trust is built through relationships, not advertisements
- Mobile money integration signals legitimacy
- Swahili-first communication shows respect and understanding

## Process
1. Map the competitive landscape (who else solves this problem?)
2. Identify what's genuinely different about your approach
3. Validate with actual customers (do they care about this difference?)
4. Express it in one sentence that a non-expert would understand
5. Test it in real conversations before committing to marketing

## Anti-Patterns in Positioning
- "We're the best" (says everyone)
- Differentiating on features that don't matter to customers
- Positioning for investors instead of users
- Ignoring local context in favor of global aspirations
