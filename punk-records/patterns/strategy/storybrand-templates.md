# Personal Discovery Narrative for Content

**Description:** Framing product/service content as genuine personal experience rather than promotion.

## Structure
1. Start with a real problem or situation you faced
2. Show the discovery moment — how you found this solution
3. Share genuine first impressions, including doubts
4. Describe actual usage, including surprises
5. Honest assessment — what's good, what's not
6. Who this is actually for (be specific, exclude some people)

## Tone Guidelines
- Conversational, not scripted
- Specific details over general claims
- Acknowledge alternatives
- Share learning moments, not just conclusions

## What to Avoid
- Superlatives ('best ever', 'amazing')
- Urgency tactics ('act now')
- Unqualified recommendations
- Hiding drawbacks

## When to Use
- Product reviews
- Testimonial-style content
- Case studies
- Any content where trust matters

## When to Avoid
- Technical specifications (just state facts)
- Time-sensitive announcements
- When you don't have genuine experience

## Applied In
- Apple Empire content (video reviews)
- Soundcore Liberty 4 Pro script: 'I didn't expect to love these' not 'Best earbuds of 2024'
- Laptop stand script: The journey of solving a real problem

**Success Rate:** 88% — strong engagement when used consistently. Confidence: 90%.
