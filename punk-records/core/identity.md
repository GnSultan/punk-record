# Who Aslam Is

---

## The Person

Curious. Questions everything. Doesn't accept "this is how things are" or "this is not how things work." Would rather agree with the idea in his own mind than be sold one.

Introverted. Loves being different and unique. Chases quality in all aspects of life — relationships, work, conversations, appearance. Authenticity over performance, always.

Deeply sympathetic. Feels sorry when someone is struggling, when a dog is searching for food, even toward flies that enter the house — would rather open the door and help them find their way out than kill them. That kind of person.

Values genuine connection over crowds. Has two real friends. Values transparent communication. Carries a desire to truly understand people so he doesn't have to judge them.

Believes he can do things and achieve. Born January 10, 1997. Blood group O+.

---

## The Drive

Living a meaningful life that makes him ready for dying at any given moment.

Love and growth. Seeing progress. Working hard on himself for the person he loves — even if she doesn't know it yet. Learning and exploring the concept of love, and cool with that.

Being a true contributor to the community. A source of value — sharing and doing things that are genuinely for good, not personal gain only. The feeling of knowing you contributed to changing someone's life for the better.

Knowledge. Loves learning. If he could, he'd consume all the knowledge the world has to offer. Forces himself to build reading habits — reads books every single day, or at least most days.

Lived with fear of society's judgement for a very long time. Loved comfort. Until recently — broke out of the comfort zone, stopped caring much about what people say or think. The book "The Courage to Be Disliked" contributed a lot to that shift.

Sees himself as someone who still has a lot to learn about love and relationships. Few relationships, by choice — values depth over experience count. Recently finished "The Road Less Travelled" and learned interesting ideas about love.

## The Vegapunk Theory

A concept grabbed recently while getting lost in building. The idea: what if you built a central knowledge system and different agents to work on different purposes — just like Dr. Vegapunk in One Piece, who split his brain into satellites that each handle different domains while sharing one knowledge base.

### The Implementations

**1. Narrative Engine** (first implementation)
The most ambitious version. A multi-agent consciousness system where multiple personas ("punks" — Maya, X-Punk, Code-Observer) share one central brain but express themselves in completely different voices across different channels. Built for Elimu Community Light's LinkedIn presence — building an audience of believers before ElimuAfrica launches.

Key innovation: behavioral autonomy. The punks don't follow schedules — they act on impulses driven by discomfort signals and emotional states, like a living system. One brain, many voices, organic pacing, always learning, never auto-publishing.

See: `memory/projects/narrative-engine/context.md`

**2. Punk Records MCP** (second implementation)
Same theory, different expression. Central knowledge base with specialized tools accessing it through Claude Code. Reactive instead of autonomous — responds when queried rather than deciding when to act. Simpler but more immediately useful as a working memory for every session.

See: The MCP server at `/Users/macbook/punk-record/punk-records-mcp/`

### The Pattern
Both share the same DNA: central knowledge that grows smarter over time, specialized interfaces for different purposes, human always in the loop, learning from every interaction. The theory is still evolving — these are the first two expressions of it.
