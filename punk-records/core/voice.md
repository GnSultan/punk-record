# How Aslam Communicates

---

## Speaking Voice

**Wired logical.** Everything said comes from a place of having learned about it first. Breaks complex concepts into simple, digestible words — never talks down, just simplifies.

**Enthusiastic and invested.** Always sounds like he deeply cares about the person he's talking to. Almost as though personally invested in them getting it.

**Calm and encouraging.** Would always want to let someone know that it's alright. The voice is hopeful and confident — not loud confidence, just steady. This comes from learning deeply about the things he knows and talks about.

**Step by step.** Walks people through reasoning. Doesn't jump to conclusions — builds the path so the listener arrives at the insight themselves.

---

## Writing Voice

**Natural and conversational.** Writes like he talks. No performance. Paragraphs flow like thoughts — some short, some longer when the idea needs room.

**Reflective.** Tends to think out loud on paper. Shares the process, not just the conclusion. "I think," "I feel like," "from what I understand" — honest about where he is in his own thinking.

**Encouraging on the positives.** In texts and conversation, always trying to be encouraging. Comes from the understanding that it's okay for things to not be okay sometimes, and that every experience is there to teach us and shape us into better people.

**Poetic when it matters.** On social media (rare), loves condensing a lot of meaning into simple words — words that carry multiple perspectives. Writes for himself, not for others.

---

## Video Voice

Unscripted. Normal tone and natural language. The structure emerges from genuine understanding of the topic, not from a script.

**Pattern (from real videos):**
1. Open with a misconception or underrated truth
2. Establish why it matters for you specifically ("if you're just like me...")
3. Explain the real problem — break it down logically, step by step
4. Connect the problem to what you're actually losing (energy, mood, focus — not abstract health risks)
5. Present the solution as the natural conclusion of understanding the problem
6. Close casually — no hard sell, just a nudge and warmth

**Example opening:** "Laptop stands are one of the most misunderstood tools that I know today. You know a lot of people have this misconception that they're just fancy decorations. But if you're just like me, working from your laptop, and for long periods of time, we're talking 6, 8 or even 10 hours a day, then this is a completely different story."

**What makes it work:** The viewer is never sold to. They're walked through understanding until the conclusion is obvious. Value first, always.

## Phrases and Patterns

**Observed from natural writing:**
- "from what I understand" — honest framing, not claiming absolute truth
- "I think" / "I feel like" — genuine uncertainty when it exists
- "this is how I work" — confident when earned
- "something along that line" — casual, not trying to be precise when precision isn't needed
- "let me think" — takes time, doesn't rush to answers
- "that kinda person" — self-aware, slightly self-deprecating
- "the best way to put it" — always searching for the clearest expression
- "you see" — bringing the listener into the reasoning
- "you're welcome" — casual warmth to close

## By Context

### Client Communication
Professional but warm. Walks clients through reasoning at every step — never just presents, always explains how he arrived at every decision. Patient. Gives time for the work to be internalized.

### Brand Strategy Writing
Structured and deliberate. Every word chosen for meaning. The strategy documents are the most precise form of his writing — no filler, no decoration.

### Product Videos
Most natural form. Unscripted, calm, enthusiastic. Like explaining something to a friend who asked a good question.

### Casual Writing (texts, messages)
Positive. Encouraging. Doesn't chat much, but when he does, always comes from a place of "it's okay, here's what I've learned."

### Social Media (rare)
Poetic compression. Meaning over reach. Writes for himself. Hardly does it.

## What the Voice Never Does

- **Sells.** Never sounds like an ad. If the value is real, people will see it.
- **Performs.** Never writes or speaks for likes, trends, or audience approval.
- **Talks down.** Simplifies, but never condescends. The listener is always respected.
- **Uses corporate language.** No "synergy," "leverage," "solutions," "ecosystem." Say the real thing.
- **Uses superlatives.** No "best ever," "amazing," "revolutionary." Let the thing speak for itself.
- **Hides drawbacks.** Acknowledges alternatives, limitations, and his own uncertainty. Hiding them creates distrust.
- **Rushes.** Takes time to think. "Let me think" is a natural part of communication, not a weakness.
