# Pulse Brand Studio Brand Identity

## Status: Discontinued

Pulse Brand Studio was Aslam's branding agency — an ambitious project that fell short because it was only one person with no companion. As he learned more, he realized he could do better as a freelancer than promise clients a design agency, which felt misleading. It didn't go well with his authenticity nature, so he quit it and became an independent freelancer (captured at imaslam.com).

The brand identity below is preserved as reference — the methodology and design principles live on in the freelance work, even though the agency entity is retired.

## Purpose

To empower the bold thinkers and dreamers who dare to disrupt the ordinary and build brands that stand out from the crowd.

## Tagline

**"Dream Big. Brand Bold."**

## Brand Story

### The Spark
Every movement starts with a spark — a desire to break free from the mundane and create something extraordinary. Pulse Brand Studio was born from the belief that every dreamer deserves a brand that stands out.

### The Mission
Ignite the potential of brands by crafting identities that challenge the ordinary.

### The Journey
Working alongside founders, creatives, and entrepreneurs who refuse to settle — transforming their ideas into brands that make an impact.

### The Impact
Brands built with purpose. Identities that inspire confidence. Movements that leave a mark.

## Logo System

### The Mark
- **Primary:** Geometric "P" letterform with a spark/spackle element
- **The spark element** represents creativity igniting — the moment an idea becomes a brand
- **Wordmark:** "Pulse" in Clash Display typeface, semi-bold

### Variations
- Logo + wordmark (primary)
- Logo mark only (social, favicon)
- Wordmark only (editorial, documents)

### Clear Space & Sizing
- Minimum clear space: height of the "P" on all sides
- Minimum size: 32px digital, 12mm print

**Pattern:** Same monochromatic-first approach as the personal portfolio (#FAFAFA/#0F0F0F) and Thabiti (#FFFFFF/#4F4754). Color is surgical — accents exist but black and white do the heavy lifting.

## Typography

### Display / Headlines: Clash Display
- Headings, hero text, brand statements
- Weight: Semi-Bold (600)
- Sizes: H1: 72px, H2: 48px, H3: 36px
- **Same font family used in Aslam's personal portfolio** — Clash Grotesk/Display is a signature choice

### Body / Text: DM Sans or Open Sans
- Body copy, descriptions, UI elements
- Weight: Regular (400)
- Sizes: Body: 18-20px, Small: 14-16px
- Line height: 1.6 for readability

### Hierarchy Rules
- Display type creates impact (large, semi-bold, commanding)
- Body type stays readable (clean, regular weight, generous line height)
- Never compete — one dominant element per layout

## Voice & Personality

### Brand Personality
1. **Confidence & Authority** — Speak with conviction. Know the craft.
2. **Approachability & Collaboration** — Partner, not vendor. Work alongside, not above.
3. **Creativity & Unconventional Thinking** — Challenge norms. Question defaults.
4. **Energy & Inspiration** — Motivate action. Don't just inform.

### Voice Characteristics
- **Confident & authoritative** — Not arrogant. Earned confidence from craft mastery.
- **Inspiring & motivational** — Every piece of communication should make the reader feel capable of something bigger.
- **Direct** — Say it clearly. No hiding behind jargon or corporate padding.
- **Bold** — Take positions. Make statements. Don't hedge.

### Tone Shifts
- Client-facing: Warm, collaborative, confident
- Portfolio/case studies: Bold, results-driven, show-don't-tell
- Social media: Energetic, punchy, provocative (in a good way)
- Internal/process: Clear, structured, efficient

## What Pulse Reveals About the Method

Pulse Brand Studio's own brand demonstrates the same principles applied to every client:

1. **Monochromatic core + surgical accent** — Black/white primary, color used sparingly and intentionally (same pattern: portfolio, Thabiti, TMs Collection, GoodmanSeason)
2. **Typography as identity** — Clash Display is the signature typeface across Pulse and the personal brand. The font IS the brand before any other element.
3. **Purpose before visuals** — Brand story, mission, and values defined before any visual decisions
4. **Confident restraint** — Bold statements delivered through clean design, not visual noise
5. **Spark/ignition motif** — The logo's spark element, "ignite" messaging, and "pulse" name all point to the same metaphor: dormant potential activated
