# TMs Collection Brand Identity

## Who TMs Collection Is

A women's fashion label founded by Tecla, celebrating uniqueness. Not mass-market clothing — a curated space for women who want pieces that reflect their true beauty, versatility, and strength.

**Founder's voice (from brand book):** "I've always loved unique clothes that make me feel like my best self. When I find a piece that's really me, I feel happier, more confident. I want all women to experience that power!"

**Brand promise:** "We founded this label to celebrate your uniqueness. To craft clothing reflecting your true beauty, versatility and strength."

## Brand Positioning

### The Strategic Process

This project went through a name evaluation. The original name "TM's Clothing Collection" was analyzed against a proposed alternative "Techla's Boutique":

**TM's Clothing Collection** (chosen):
- Short, simple, easy to remember
- Initials lend modern, clean minimalism
- Tradeoff: initials alone don't convey brand personality

**Techla's Boutique** (proposed alternative):
- Chic, sophisticated, exudes stylish femininity
- "Boutique" communicates curated, high-end experience
- Tradeoff: personal name limits future brand flexibility

The client chose to keep TMs Collection — the minimalism of the initials aligned with their vision.

### Target Audience
Women who value:
- Unique, non-mass-market clothing
- Quality fabrics and craftsmanship
- Personal expression through fashion
- A curated shopping experience that feels intimate, not transactional

### Brand Values
1. **Uniqueness** — "Be Unique" is the central message
2. **Empowerment** — Clothing as confidence
3. **Craftsmanship** — Finest fabrics, skilled hands, quality stitching
4. **Versatility** — Unlimited possibilities in what you can wear
5. **Community** — "A sisterhood that believes your beauty, spirit and style deserve to shine"

## Color System

| Name | Hex | Role |
|------|-----|------|
| Earthy Brown | #4C4239 | Primary — natural, organic, refined, warm |
| Vibrant Peach | #F9D4B0 | Accent — femininity, warmth, brightness |

**Rationale:** Originally proposed as neutral brown, shifted to earthy brown (#4C4239) for a more natural, organic aesthetic. The peach accent (#F9D4B0) adds femininity and warmth without competing. Together they create a refined yet warm aesthetic — organic and artisan with a pop of brightness.

**This is a different approach from the monochromatic work** (portfolio, Thabiti). Here, the two-color palette serves the brand's femininity and warmth. The earthy brown grounds it; the peach lifts it.

## Typography

### Display / Logo: Arthead
- Feminine script typeface
- Used for headers, logo, titles
- Handcrafted yet polished feel
- Aligns with the curated, boutique aesthetic

### Body: Lato
- Clean, modern sans-serif
- Paragraph text and general content
- Provides readability contrast against the stylized script
- Lets the display type stand out

**Pattern:** Script for personality + sans-serif for readability. Two typefaces with clearly separated roles.

## Logo System

Three concepts were explored:

### Concept 1 (Typography-focused):
Bold Arthead wordmark in peach for "TM's" with "Collection" subtly underneath. Logo relies on typography alone to convey brand identity — elegant, high-fashion look.

### Concept 2 (Icon + Type):
Clothing hanger icon in peach wrapping around the TM's Collection name. Handwritten font for organic, approachable feel. Directly nods to fashion design.

### Concept 3 (Handwritten):
Simple handwritten wordmark with a line separating "TM's" and "Collection." Friendly, informal vibe through script aesthetics.

**All concepts aimed for:** Luxe yet warm and inviting.

## What This Project Teaches About Brand Building

### Different from Thabiti and Portfolio
This is a completely different design language from the monochromatic, typography-led work on Thabiti and the personal portfolio. TMs Collection required:
- **Two-color warmth** instead of single-hue restraint
- **Script typography** instead of geometric sans-serif
- **Femininity and softness** instead of confident minimalism
- **Community tone** instead of direct/efficient tone
- **Aspirational warmth** instead of quiet confidence

### Shared Principles Across All Brand Work
Despite the visual differences, the same underlying approach:
1. **Start with research** — Name analysis, positioning, audience understanding before any design
2. **Color has meaning** — Earthy brown for organic authenticity, peach for feminine warmth. Every color choice has a reason.
3. **Typography carries personality** — Arthead script says "boutique, feminine, crafted." The font IS the brand voice visually.
4. **Logo concepts explore range** — Three directions (type-only, icon+type, handwritten) to find the right fit
5. **Physical applications matter** — The brand lives on bags, labels, caps, cards — not just screens
6. **Founder story is central** — The "why" comes from a real person with real motivation
