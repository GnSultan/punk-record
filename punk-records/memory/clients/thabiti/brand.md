# Thabiti Brand Identity

## Who Thabiti Is

Thabiti is a character, not a corporation. The brand is personified as "James" — a 35-year-old man in Arusha who drives a clean 2019 off-white Toyota Corolla.

What people say about James:
- "If he says 9am, he's there at 8:55"
- "You can call him at 11pm. He'll pick up"
- "He just solves things. Quietly. Efficiently"
- "You trust him with your house keys"

**The Decision Filter:** "Would James do this?" If yes, do it. If no, don't.

## Why Thabiti Exists

Thabiti watched friends struggle — locals and expats alike. Unreliable transport. Moving around shouldn't be this hard. So they built what they couldn't find: reliable vehicles, transparent pricing, something that actually works.

## The Four Promises

1. **WE ANSWER** — WhatsApp/phone in under 15 minutes, 24/7. Not voicemail. A human.
2. **WE SHOW UP** — 9am means 8:55am. Roadside assistance within 30 minutes. Replacement same day.
3. **NO SURPRISES** — Price you see is the price you pay. All costs upfront. Every charge explained.
4. **WE FIX IT** — Something breaks? We solve it. Our fault? Refund + replacement. Benefit of doubt to customer.

## Typography

### Display / Headlines: Bombard Grotesk
- Wordmark, main headlines, hero text
- Weights: Regular, Medium, Bold
- **Lowercase only** — confidence through restraint
- No separate icon or symbol in the logo

### Body / Text: DM Sans
- Body copy, descriptions, UI elements
- Weights: Regular (400), Medium (500)

## Logo System

- **Primary mark**: "thabiti" wordmark in Bombard Grotesk, lowercase
- **No separate icon/symbol** — the wordmark is the logo
- Confidence through restraint
- Clean, simple — like James' car badge

## The Asterisk

The asterisk (*) is Thabiti's signature visual element.

**Meaning:**
- Radiating paths = unlimited roads, diverse directions
- Center point = the driver
- Lines extending outward = all possible journeys
- Multiple paths = freedom, flexibility, choice

**Usage:**
- Hero sections — large, subtle (20-30% opacity) behind text
- Brand pattern — repeating grid for texture/backgrounds
- Accent element — small detail on cards, materials
- Visual punctuation — "Book it. Drive it.*"

**Variations:**
- Single asterisk: large, used as message portrayal
- Pattern grid: repeating, light texture
- Detail: small, corner accent

## Visual Principles

1. **Clean, Not Minimal** — Organized and clear, not cold or inaccessible
2. **Functional, Not Decorative** — Every element serves a purpose
3. **Confident, Not Flashy** — Quiet confidence, like James
4. **Local, Not Generic** — Real Tanzania, real situations
5. **Human, Not Corporate** — Show people, faces, real interactions

### Modern Muted Aesthetic
- Feeling: 6pm in Dar es Salaam
- Texture: Matte, fabric, soft
- Sound: Whisper, not shout
- Weight: Light but grounded

### Design Execution
- Generous white space
- Restrained color use
- Typography does the work
- Subtle over loud
- Breathing room everywhere

## Voice: Talk Like James

**Principles:**
- **Clear** — Say what you mean, no jargon
- **Brief** — Respect people's time
- **Helpful** — Give answers, not runarounds
- **Warm** — Genuine, not fake-friendly
- **Direct** — No corporate hiding

### Before/After Examples

**Customer inquiry:**
- Corporate: "Thank you for your interest in our premium vehicle fleet. Please provide your travel dates, preferred vehicle category, and desired pickup location..."
- Thabiti: "Hey! When do you need it and for how long? I'll check what we have and give you the price."

**Insurance explanation:**
- Corporate: "Our comprehensive insurance package includes collision damage waiver with reduced liability exposure..."
- Thabiti: "If something happens, you're covered up to TZS 500,000. We handle everything above that. Most people never pay anything."

**Vehicle breakdown:**
- Corporate: "We regret to inform you that your vehicle has experienced a mechanical malfunction. Please contact our roadside assistance hotline..."
- Thabiti: "Sorry, that shouldn't have happened. Where are you? I'm sending someone now — should be there in 30 minutes."

### Writing Checklist
Before sending any message:
1. Would James say this? (Character test)
2. Is this actually helpful? (Value test)
3. Could I say this in fewer words? (Brevity test)
4. Am I hiding behind corporate language? (Directness test)
5. Would I want to receive this? (Empathy test)

All 5 = yes → Send it. Any = no → Rewrite.

## Swahili Integration

Use naturally, not performatively:
- Natural: "Karibu" (welcome), "Pole" (sympathetic), "Asante" (thank you), "Usijali" (don't worry)
- Mixed: "The RAV4 is booked, lakini I have a Corolla available"
- Avoid: Forced translation, tourist-brochure proverbs, code-switching when English is clearer
- Rule: If James would say it in Swahili, we do. If it feels forced, we don't.

## What We Never Do

- Ignore calls/messages
- Blame customers
- Use corporate speak
- Make excuses
- Surprise fees
- Rent vehicles we wouldn't drive
