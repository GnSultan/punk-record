# Elimu Community Light (ECOLI) Brand Identity

## What Elimu Is

Elimu Community Light is a non-profit organization dedicated to empowering underprivileged communities through early childhood development, education, and livelihood initiatives. It removes obstacles and opens doors so communities can access opportunities for growth across generations.

**This is a Pulse Brand Studio client project** — Aslam designed the full brand identity system.

## Brand Purpose

To remove obstacles so underprivileged communities can access potentials and achieve development.

## Vision

Empowered and developed communities.

## Mission

To bridge underprivileged communities with opportunities through early childhood development, education, and livelihood initiatives.

## Programs

1. **Early Childhood Development** — Nurturing young children's health, nutrition, early learning, security and safety, and responsive care-giving for underprivileged communities
2. **Education** — Initiatives to bridge gaps and facilitate access to quality primary and secondary education
3. **Livelihoods** — Programs that equip youth and women with vocational skills, entrepreneurship training, and pathways to economic empowerment

## Logo System

### The Mark
- **Symbol:** Geometric figure with raised arms in celebration — representing triumph and empowerment
- **Wordmark:** "elimu" in Poppins, lowercase
- **Combined:** Symbol + wordmark lockup (preferred usage)

### Logo Direction
The logo combines "elimu" (Swahili for knowledge/empowerment) with a figure raising arms in celebration. The simple, geometric figure is inclusive — representing people from all walks of life. Upward angles and raised arms convey achievement, joy, and empowerment.

### Logo Variants
- Full lockup (symbol + wordmark) — primary
- Logomark only (raised figure) — social, favicon
- Wordmark only — editorial contexts

### Usage Rules
- Clear space: height of the "e" in the wordmark on all sides
- Minimum size: 1 inch width (full lockup), 0.5 inches (logomark)
- Primary: brand purple. One-color versions in black or white acceptable
- Never stretch, condense, rotate, or modify proportions

## Color System

| Name | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|------|-----|-----|-------|
| Royal Purple | #7862F7 | 120, 98, 247 | Primary brand color, logo, headers, CTAs |
| Mint Green | #98FB98 | 152, 251, 152 | Accent — icons, illustrations, backgrounds |
| White | #FFFFFF | 255, 255, 255 | Primary background |
| Light Gray | #F5F5F5 | 245, 245, 245 | Subtle background, containers |
| Black | #000000 | 0, 0, 0 | Body text, secondary accent |

### Color Psychology
- **Royal Purple** — Luxury, nobility, sophistication. Represents aspirational vision of empowerment and unlocking human potential. Conveys inspiration and achievement.
- **Mint Green** — Growth, freshness, new beginnings. Energizing accent aligned with creating positive change.
- **Black & White** — Grounding neutrals for legibility and crispness.
- **Light Gray** — Calming background that lets content and colors stand out.

### Usage Rules
- Purple is primary but avoid overusing in large amounts (can appear too vibrant)
- Mint green for accents only — needs white/gray balance when paired with purple
- White as primary background for print and digital
- Light gray for containers when white feels too harsh
- Never use gray as primary text color

## Typography

### Font Family: Poppins
- **Headings:** Poppins Medium
- **Body:** Poppins Light
- **CTAs:** Poppins Semi-Bold
- **Quotes/Testimonials:** Poppins Light Italic

### Key Rule
**Only use lowercase letters for both headings and body copy** — promotes friendliness and approachability. This is a defining typographic choice for the brand.

### Hierarchy
- H1 (Primary Headings): Poppins Medium — page titles, hero headers, section headers
- H2-H4 (Secondary Headings): Poppins Medium — sub-headers, emphasis within body
- Body Copy: Poppins Light — body text, lists, descriptions
- CTAs: Poppins Semi-Bold — buttons, links, prompts
- Quotes: Poppins Light Italic — testimonials, callouts

## Brand Experience

### What People Should Feel
Embraced by possibility, hope, and inspiration. Passion, optimism, and uplifting energy. Grounding straightforwardness that feels supportive, empowering, and committed. Spark belief in one's abilities while feeling included in a caring community.

### Digital Experience
- **Website:** Clean, content-driven design. Prominent CTAs, powerful visuals/videos, solutions-focused narrative. Immersive animations and interactions underscoring empowerment.
- **Social Media:** Inspiring visual storytelling through photos/videos celebrating human potential. Consistent linking back to elimu.org content hub.

### Photography Style
- **Style:** Authentic, unstylized, documentary-style. Real, unscripted moments. "No personas" approach. Visual narratives of communities being empowered.
- **Lighting:** Natural light prioritized. Warm, golden quality — optimistic and vibrant.
- **Subjects:** Diverse groups across gender, age, and ethnicity. Respectful and dignified. Subjects as active participants, not models.
- **Composition:** Dynamic angles, tight crops alongside wider environmental scenes. Candid expressions over staged portraits.
- **Color Treatment:** Warm, moderate color grade. High contrast, deep blacks. Bright, saturated feel. Minor grain/texture for documentary aesthetic.

### Videography
- **Content:** B-roll majority. Verite scenes showcasing work and impact. Interviews and processes supplement.
- **Technique:** Smooth handheld + stabilized shots. Camera whips, focus pulls, perspective changes.
- **Audio:** Location sound with inspirational instrumentals/vocals for key moments.
- **Editing:** Rhythmic, visually-driven. Let stories breathe. Concise titles and graphical elements.
- **Distribution:** Optimized for Instagram/Facebook feeds, YouTube long-form, website/email, and TV-style presentations.

## What This Brand Reveals About the Method

This is Pulse Brand Studio's most **purpose-driven** brand identity. Key differences from commercial brands:

1. **Color with meaning** — Royal purple (#7862F7) is bold and aspirational, not muted or restrained. This is the most colorful primary palette across all brands — because the mission demands visibility and hope, not quiet confidence.
2. **Lowercase everything** — A deliberate typographic rule that makes the brand feel approachable and non-hierarchical. Different from Thabiti's lowercase-by-confidence — here it's lowercase-by-inclusion.
3. **Documentary visual approach** — Photography and videography guidelines are the most detailed of any brand, because for an NGO, authentic visuals ARE the brand.
4. **Figure symbol** — The raised-arms figure is the only brand with a representational symbol (vs Thabiti's wordmark-only, TMs Collection's monogram, GoodmanSeason's abstract sun). An NGO needs a human element in the mark itself.
