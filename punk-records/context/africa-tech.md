# Africa Tech Ecosystem Context

## The Landscape

Africa's tech ecosystem is not "behind" — it's different. Solutions that emerge here often leapfrog Western approaches because they're built for real constraints, not theoretical ones.

## Key Realities

**Mobile-First (Mobile-Only):**
- Most Africans access the internet through phones, not computers
- Feature phones still have significant market share in some regions
- Data costs are a real barrier — every MB matters
- USSD and SMS remain viable channels for reaching users

**Payment Innovation:**
- Mobile money (M-Pesa, etc.) leapfrogged traditional banking
- Cash-to-digital bridge is well-established
- Credit card penetration is low — don't build for it
- Payment integration is a trust signal

**Connectivity Challenges:**
- Reliable broadband is urban luxury
- 3G/4G coverage is patchy outside cities
- Offline-capable solutions aren't nice-to-have, they're necessary
- Solutions that degrade gracefully win

**Talent & Community:**
- Growing developer communities across the continent
- Brain drain is real — competitive salaries matter
- Developer conferences and meetups are growing
- Open source contributions are increasing

## Opportunities

- Education technology (massive underserved market)
- Agricultural technology (70%+ of population)
- Health technology (distributed healthcare needs)
- Financial inclusion (unbanked and underbanked populations)
- Local content creation (demand for relevant, local content)

## Anti-Patterns in African Tech

- Building "Africa versions" of Western products without understanding local context
- Assuming mobile money is "just another payment method" (it's infrastructure)
- Ignoring offline requirements
- Building in English only for multilingual populations
- Venture capital-driven metrics that don't map to local market dynamics
