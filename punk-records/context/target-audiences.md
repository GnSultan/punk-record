# Target Audiences — Who We Build For

---

## ElimuAfrica: Tanzanian Students

**Primary:** Secondary school students (Form 1-4) in Tanzania

**Their Reality:**
- Shared devices at school, possibly no device at home
- Intermittent or no internet connectivity
- Swahili as primary language, English as school language
- Teacher-to-student ratios that make individual attention impossible
- Mathematics and science feel abstract without practical tools
- Exam pressure is intense — failing means limited opportunities

**What They Need:**
- Content that works without internet
- Explanations in Swahili that actually make sense (not translations)
- Interactive experiences that make abstract concepts tangible
- Practice with immediate, useful feedback
- Confidence, not just knowledge

---

## Apple Empire: Phone Buyers in Tanzania

**Primary:** Young professionals and tech-curious consumers in Dar es Salaam

**Their Reality:**
- A phone is their primary computer, camera, entertainment, and communication tool
- Making the wrong choice wastes significant money
- Overwhelmed by marketing claims from every brand
- Trust recommendations from people they relate to over ads
- Price-conscious but willing to pay for genuinely good products

**What They Need:**
- Honest guidance from someone who actually uses the products
- Real comparisons, not sponsored endorsements
- Understanding of what matters for their specific use case
- Confidence that they're making a good choice

---

## Brand Clients: Tanzanian Businesses

**Primary:** Small-to-medium Tanzanian businesses seeking brand differentiation

**Their Reality:**
- Often skeptical of "branding" as a concept (feels like luxury)
- Competing against larger, better-funded competitors
- Marketing often means "post on Instagram" without strategy
- Word-of-mouth is their most powerful channel
- Budget-conscious — need to see ROI

**What They Need:**
- Proof that brand strategy drives real business results
- A clear "why" that resonates with their customers
- Messaging they can actually use (not abstract brand books)
- Visual identity that stands out in their local market
- Practical implementation, not just beautiful decks

---

## International Tourists (via Client Work)

**Primary:** International travelers seeking authentic Tanzanian experiences

**Their Reality:**
- Tired of cookie-cutter safari packages
- Seeking authenticity but don't know how to find it
- Nervous about logistics in an unfamiliar country
- Willing to pay premium for genuine local knowledge
- Research online but trust personal stories over marketing

**What They Need:**
- Confidence in their travel partner's local expertise
- Authentic stories from real guides and travelers
- Practical information that addresses their real fears
- An experience that feels personal, not packaged
