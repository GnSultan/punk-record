# Tanzania Market Context

---

## Education System

**Key Facts:**
- 75% of Tanzanian students fail mathematics (high confidence — national examination statistics)
- Teacher-to-student ratios are extremely high in public schools
- Many schools have limited or no internet connectivity
- English-language instruction creates barriers for Swahili-speaking students
- Limited or shared computer access in schools

**Implications:**
- Technology solutions must work offline
- Solutions must scale beyond teacher availability
- Swahili-native content is essential
- Low-bandwidth and shared-device assumptions are necessary

---

## Tech Ecosystem

**Key Facts:**
- Mobile money (M-Pesa) is widely adopted — cash is less common for digital transactions
- WhatsApp is the dominant communication platform for personal and business use
- Smartphone adoption is growing but data costs remain a barrier
- Local tech talent exists but often leaves for international opportunities

**Implications:**
- Payment integration should prioritize mobile money
- WhatsApp integration can be a distribution channel
- Apps should minimize data usage
- Local hiring requires competitive positioning against global companies

---

## Infrastructure Reality

- Internet is expensive, unreliable, and often unavailable outside major cities
- Power outages are common — battery life and data persistence matter
- Most users access the internet through mobile phones, not computers
- Shared devices are the norm in schools and homes

---

## Cultural Context

- Swahili is the language of daily life; English is the language of formal education
- Trust is built through relationships, not advertisements
- Word-of-mouth through WhatsApp is the primary recommendation channel
- Community and family influence purchasing and adoption decisions
